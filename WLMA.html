Why Learn Martial Arts?

Physical benefits of Martial Arts

Vladisavljevic (2021) explains the benefits of martial arts below:

  • Weight loss and muscle gain: results from regular training such as practicing martial arts techniques or gym workouts.
  • Flexibility: results from honing martial art techniques such as high kicks, spinning strikes.
  • Agility and reflexes: results from training which build in muscles a fast-twitch fiber; furthermore, training in hand to hand combat leads to the evolution of reflexes.

Martial Arts and Intelligence

According to Black Belt Trek (2023), martial art is considered as attention state training which boasts someone's focus; next, martial arts leads to a quick thinking and response; as a consequence, it leads to a better academic performance (Basingstoke Martial Arts Academy, 2023); finally, martial arts improves someone's memory. Critical thinking is another benefit of martial arts because it teaches the player to anticipate the opponent's next move; moreover, martial arts refines someone's decision-making skills which boasts someone's confidence; indeed, a karate or aikido session makes someone more intuitive (Monster Martial Arts, 2017; Evolve MMA, 2023). Kahn (2022) states that martial arts improves someone's emotional intelligence such as

  • Forms: requires motion which enhances the person's spatial awareness and intuition by exercising.
  • Breath Work: martial arts requires a person to control their breathing; as a result, the ability to self-regulate improves.
By learning new skills and controlling thoughts and emotions, the emotional intelligence of students increases; as a result,they become better at communication skills ( Vladisavljevic, 2023). Martial arts also reduces aggressive behaviour in boys; as a result, there is a high possibility that these boys save someone from bullying (Johnstone, 2018).

Other Benefits of Martial Arts

Martial arts like meditation and breathing reduce stress (Master Shu Yu, 2023). Ronin Training Center (2021) stated some of the benefits of martial arts below:

  1. Energy: Regular training increase the blood flow which improves the cardiovascular system by moving oxygen through the blood; as a result, it increases the possibility of sleeping at night and be more active during the day.
  2. Mental Balance: martial artists gain a composed demeanor by developing what is called "a sense of peace" which stems from believing in their training.
  3. End Harmful Habits: it is common for someone who started martial arts to abstain from drinking and smoking.
  4. Mental Health: regular exercise alleviates symptoms of depression and anxiety.
  5. Reflexes and Coordination: with practicing martial arts, someone needs to expect weight loss and improved cardiovascular health.
  6. Career: Martial arts teach self-discipline, improve focus, and require persistence and confidence; as a result, the eligibility to be hired and accepted by employers is more likely (Horizon Martial Arts West Seneca, 2022).
  7. Friendship: When martial artists train with others in a class, the likelihood of building a bond of friendship which is built communication and teamwork increases due to them sharing similar ideals and goals (Garcia, 2021a).
  8. Family: When a family practices martial arts a bond filled with lifelong memories; moreover, the bond that family shares is built by mutual encouragement between parents and their kids (Master S.H Yu, 2023).
  9. Personal Development: martial arts teaches someone to self-reflect which improves their critical thinking skills by understanding what kind of obstacles they are facing (Brennan, 2021).

Drawbacks of Learning Martial Arts

Some Martial Arts carry risks; for instance, According to sportsver (2023) disadvantage of a martial art like Karate are

  • The cost of a class is between $100-$150 per week which is expensive for players who want to compete.
  • Time consuming because it is an all-year round sport
  • Some people can take advantage of their knowledge in karate and bully other people.
  • The belt colours are not a measure of skill.

Furthermore, TaekwondoKing (2023) lists some advantages of Taekwondo such as

  • The gear is expensive
  • Finding a place to learn taekwondo is difficult.
  • You need to be always with an instructor.
  • it is a martial art that pushes pressure on the joints.
  • It is challenging to be a taekwondo master.

Learning More Than One Style

Learning different types of martial arts depends on the person; for example, some people can find the idea helpful to their training, confidence, and health and others might find it challenging (Performace Jiujitsu, 2023). Learning multiple martial arts is disadvantageous because it hard for beginners because they increase the amount of training by a double; furthermore, the focus of training can be different from the other; as a result, you are training two different muscles; finally, they affect someone's progress in training (Starpo, 2022).

Conclusion

As much as sports like karate and taekwondo carry some risks martial is still worth to be practiced; for instance, Javatpoint (2021) lists some martial artists who are also successful actors such as Bruce Lee and Wesley Snipes; furthermore, martial arts has developed into a sport managed by high organisations such as the UFC and the World Open Martial Arts Championship. The Ronin Training Center(2021) states how martial arts are valuable to someone; for example, they improve someone's focus and decision-making skills; moreover, martial arts excercise help in reducing cardiovascular problems.Next, for those interested in martial arts, China and Thailand are possible choices. Martial arts form healthy relationship within a family; furthermore, the relationship become stronger because when you train, you might have colleagues who share similar goals (Garcia, 2021). Finally, according to Starpo (2022), it is better to focus on one martial art.
